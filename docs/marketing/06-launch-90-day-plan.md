# Hearth — 90-Day Launch Plan

The first 90 days of marketing execution. Concrete, week-by-week, with owners and outputs.

**Companion docs:** [04-hearth-marketing-strategy.md](./04-hearth-marketing-strategy.md), [05-content-engine.md](./05-content-engine.md)

## Pre-flight (week -2 and -1)

These two weeks happen before launch day. Without them, week 1 is chaos.

### Week -2 — Set up

- [ ] Substack publication created. Branding applied (Fraunces title, paper background, ember accent). About page written.
- [ ] Instagram account created. Bio rewritten to the new positioning. Profile photo finalized. 3 highlight covers designed (Embers / Keepers / Stories).
- [ ] LinkedIn — founder profile rewritten to lead with Hearth. Banner designed. About section published.
- [ ] YouTube channel created. Banner, profile photo, about section. One channel-trailer video queued.
- [ ] Threads, Pinterest, Bluesky accounts created and brand-locked. No posting yet.
- [ ] Remotion codebase set up per [02-remotion-capabilities.md](./02-remotion-capabilities.md). Brand tokens file imported. First template Reel rendered as smoke test.
- [ ] Asset library v0 — 20 b-roll clips, 5 ambient music tracks, 4 ElevenLabs voice profiles, 50 quote cards from existing Embers.
- [ ] Analytics — Plausible installed on hearth site. UTM convention documented. Substack analytics access.
- [ ] Email infrastructure — Substack handles the Letter; a transactional service (Resend) handles intake confirmations and Keeper match emails.
- [ ] Atom calendar — first 13 weeks of atoms outlined at the topic level. First 4 atoms drafted by the founder + Lead Keeper.

### Week -1 — Stage

- [ ] First atom published as a "soft launch" Letter to a friends-and-family list of 50.
- [ ] Three Reels rendered and queued in Buffer.
- [ ] First IG carousel essay scheduled for launch day +1.
- [ ] One YouTube long essay rendered and uploaded as Unlisted, ready to go public.
- [ ] Founder writes 5 LinkedIn posts in advance.
- [ ] Hearth Letter signup widget added to every page of dearhearth.com.
- [ ] Exit popup confirmed live with $10-off + gift framing.
- [ ] One last test order through intake → first Sit booking → Long Talk first message. End-to-end.

## Month 1 — Plant the flag (weeks 1–4)

The job of month 1: get the **Letter** off the ground and make the brand voice unmissable on Instagram. No paid spend. No PR push yet. Slow and clear.

### Week 1

- **Mon:** Hearth.com goes public. Founder posts the founding-story Letter on Substack. Founder posts the same story (reframed first-person) on LinkedIn. Twitter/X founder thread.
- **Tue:** First IG Reel — whisper monologue from the founder (the "I built this for the people I called when I was the one everyone called" essay).
- **Wed:** YouTube long essay drops — the founding story as a 12-minute visual essay. Footage: hands, doorways, kitchens, a chair being pulled out.
- **Thu:** IG Reel — letter-to pattern. "A letter to anyone who's been the family's emotional infrastructure for fifteen years."
- **Fri:** Pinterest — 5 quote pins from the Letter.
- **Sat:** IG Reel — slow b-roll voiceover. Closing paragraph of the Letter.
- **Sun:** Atom #2 publishes — Lead Keeper writes about the difference between *holding* and *fixing*.
- **Daily:** Substack Notes (1–2/day), Threads (3/day), IG Stories (2–3/day).

### Week 2

- Continue the rhythm. Letter, 11 outputs.
- **Outreach:** Founder reaches out to 10 podcast hosts in the wellness/diaspora space — the founding story pitch. Goal: 2 yeses.
- **First seed:** Send the first Letter to 50 hand-picked early subscribers (friends who fit the SVA, founder's network). Personal note on each.
- **Embers cross-link:** Update each existing Embers essay on dearhearth.com to invite readers to the Letter.

### Week 3

- Continue rhythm.
- **First "feature" Letter** — a member story (with consent and honorarium). This is the highest-converting type of atom; do one in month 1 to model the format.
- **Outreach to 5 newsletter editors** in the diaspora wellness space (MasalaChai, Brown Girl Magazine, The Juggernaut). Pitch: a sponsored guest essay in Hearth's voice.
- **First analytics review.** Subs growth, open rate, Reel saves. No decisions yet — three data points isn't a trend.

### Week 4

- Continue rhythm.
- **First experiment:** Test pure-text-on-cream-paper Reels (no faces, no voiceover) for 2 of the week's 4 Reels. Measure saves vs. whisper-monologue baseline.
- **First member-acquired-from-content** target: 5 paid sign-ups in month 1. Most will be from the Letter footnote, not from social.

**Month 1 targets:**
- 500 Letter subscribers
- 50%+ open rate
- 4 weekly Reels averaging > 5,000 views
- 1 YouTube essay > 2,000 views
- 5 paid members (any source)

## Month 2 — Find the format (weeks 5–8)

The job of month 2: figure out which Reel format is *yours.* Double down on it. Start the partnerships engine.

### Week 5

- Continue rhythm.
- **Second member story Letter.** Different SVA archetype this time (e.g., "the man who was never taught to grieve").
- **Founder podcast appearance #1** — record. Goal is a 60-min conversation that becomes 6 pieces of derivative content.

### Week 6

- Continue rhythm.
- **First sponsored newsletter placement.** $2K budget. Write a full editorial essay for the host newsletter, with a soft CTA at the end. Track signups via a dedicated UTM and a vanity URL.
- **Begin Lead Keeper LinkedIn posting** — Aruna or whoever else is on the team starts posting weekly under their own name, signed "Keeper at Hearth."

### Week 7

- Continue rhythm.
- **First seasonal atom** (timed to whatever cultural moment is closest — Mother's Day, Eid, etc.).
- **Reels format decision.** By end of week 7, we have ~30 Reels of data. Identify the top 3 by saves + DM shares. Codify the format. Make it half of next month's Reel output.

### Week 8

- Continue rhythm.
- **Second podcast recording.** Different host, different audience.
- **First Pinterest analytics review.** Are pins driving Letter signups? If yes, scale to 10 pins/week. If no, drop to 2/week and re-evaluate next month.

**Month 2 targets:**
- 1,500 Letter subscribers (net month gain: 1,000)
- 50%+ open rate sustained
- 2 podcast appearances live
- 1 sponsored newsletter placement converted at $80 CAC or better
- 12 paid members (cumulative)
- A Reels format identified as "Hearth's signature"

## Month 3 — Compounding (weeks 9–12)

The job of month 3: turn the Letter into a real audience asset. Open the founder LinkedIn engine. Begin the upgrade play to Hearth Deep.

### Week 9

- Continue rhythm.
- **Founder LinkedIn cadence locks.** 5 posts/week minimum. Mix: 1 long-form essay, 2 short observational posts, 1 carousel, 1 video.
- **First "Hearth Letter Anthology" YouTube video** — read the 4 best Letters of the past 8 weeks as a single 35-min audio essay over slow visuals. Cornerstone content for slow brand-building.

### Week 10

- Continue rhythm.
- **Begin upgrade engine.** Identify Hearthside members who've booked an extra Sit twice in the last 30 days. Their Keeper sends a hand-written email suggesting Hearth Deep. Track conversion rate.
- **Third podcast recording.**
- **First member testimonial video.** With consent + payment. Use the testimonial Reel format identified in month 2.

### Week 11

- Continue rhythm.
- **First paid retargeting campaign.** Audience: people who visited dearhearth.com from the Letter or social but didn't subscribe. Budget: $500. Creative: best-performing organic Reel. Goal: validate that paid retargeting works for Hearth.
- **Second sponsored newsletter** — different newsletter, same playbook.
- **Lead Keeper LinkedIn cadence locks.** 2 posts/week.

### Week 12

- Continue rhythm.
- **End-of-quarter review.** Pull the metrics from [04-hearth-marketing-strategy.md](./04-hearth-marketing-strategy.md). Honest assessment: are we tracking? What's working? What's a money pit?
- **Plan month 4 atom calendar** at the topic level.
- **Ship a quarter-in-review Letter** — 1,500 words on what you've learned in 90 days. This becomes its own Letter and converts skeptics.

**Month 3 targets:**
- 3,500 Letter subscribers
- 55%+ open rate (depth of engagement, not just volume)
- 35 paid members (cumulative)
- First Hearthside → Hearth Deep upgrade documented
- 3 podcast appearances live
- $40 blended CAC across organic + light retargeting

## What success looks like at day 90

- A Letter that ships every Sunday and people actually open
- A signature Reel format that saves at top of category
- A YouTube long-form video that's already cumulatively > 50,000 minutes watched
- 35–50 paid members
- A founder who is recognisably Hearth-aligned in public, but not the only voice
- An atom calendar planned 13 weeks ahead, with at least 4 written in advance
- A working hand-off between Author → Content Lead → Channels
- A Keeper or two who can write Letters

---

## What failure looks like at day 90 (and what to do about it)

- **Letter open rate < 35%.** The voice isn't landing or you've over-recruited curious-but-uncommitted subscribers. Fix: write 4 weeks of more specific atoms, prune the list.
- **Reels averaging < 1,000 views.** The format isn't right or the hook is weak. Fix: study the top 3 Reels in the category that you're not, and adapt one element at a time.
- **Intake started but < 10% conversion to paid.** Friction in payment, weak match-promise, or no-confidence-in-Keeper. Fix: add 2 more Keeper profiles to the public site, redo the "what happens after you pay" email sequence.
- **Paid member churn > 30% by month 2.** The product isn't matching the marketing. Marketing pause until product team can identify the gap.
- **Founder burnout.** The single highest-severity failure mode for any solo-founder marketing engine. Fix: hire the Content Lead earlier than month 6.

## After day 90

The 90-day plan is not the strategy. The strategy is in [04-hearth-marketing-strategy.md](./04-hearth-marketing-strategy.md). After day 90, return to that strategy, look at the 12-month targets, and back-cast a 30-day plan for month 4 against those targets.

The atomic content engine continues. The Letter continues. The voice does not change.

We are building the slow, durable thing.
