# Hearth Platform Strategy

A 2026 playbook for marketing a sensitive, slow, literary peer-support membership across ten channels. Voice: warm, slow, literary. Promise: "Pull up a chair. You weren't meant to carry it alone." Product: a $39/$99 monthly membership pairing members with a trained Keeper (not a therapist) for long-term emotional support.

This document is written for the operator who has to ship content next week. It moves from platform-level mechanics to a unifying weekly content engine.

---

## 1. Instagram Reels — the primary growth lever

### Algorithm dynamics (2026)

Adam Mosseri confirmed in early 2026 that the three load-bearing ranking signals for Reels are, in order: **watch time, likes-per-reach, and DM shares**. Watch time is the dominant signal — completion plus replays beats every other metric. Saves and "sends to a friend" are now weighted more than likes. Instagram's 2026 "Originality Score" actively de-ranks recycled content; visible TikTok watermarks trigger automatic reach suppression. The 2026 redesign placed Reels front and center on the home screen, which means a tighter feed and less room for under-performing posts.

### Optimal length, aspect ratio, format

- 9:16 vertical, 1080×1920, 30fps minimum
- 7–15 seconds for awareness/scroll-stoppers
- 60–90 seconds for emotional storytelling — Hearth's primary lane
- Loop the last frame back into the first to drive completion above 100%
- Always burn captions onto the video (sound-off viewing is still ~60% of plays)
- Cover image must be designed for the new grid — readable at 1:1 thumb size

### The 3 content formats that perform best for emotional / wellness brands

1. **The whisper monologue.** Founder or Keeper, soft-lit, talking quietly to camera in a domestic setting (kitchen, window seat, wool blanket). 60–75 seconds. One idea, slowly delivered. Lower-third caption with a single sentence the viewer can screenshot. This is what Brené Brown and Sahaj Kohli ride.
2. **The "letter to" pattern.** Text-on-screen format that begins "A letter to anyone who…" and then reveals one tender truth per beat. Works because it borrows the intimacy of a handwritten note inside a 12-second container. High save rate.
3. **Slow b-roll + voiceover essay.** Non-faces — hands holding a mug, rain on a window, a chair being pulled out, a candle. Voiceover reads a 90-second passage. This is the format Hearth was *built* for visually. Mimics the texture of On Being, Cup of Jo, and the Penguin Random House literary Reels.

### Hook patterns (first 3 seconds)

- "If you've ever cried in a car, this is for you."
- "Nobody told me grief had a second wave."
- "A letter to the version of you that's holding it together for everyone else."
- "Three things I wish someone had said to me at 2 a.m."
- Pattern interrupt: a quiet image + a single sentence on-screen, no music. In a feed of dopamine, stillness is the hook.
- Avoid: "Hi guys!" intros, jump-cut question barrages, finger-pointing meme overlays. They read as therapy-influencer cosplay and de-rank under the originality score.

### Audio / music conventions

- Use Instagram's **Original Audio** tag whenever possible — the algorithm gives a meaningful boost to original sound, and original audio compounds (every remix points back to you).
- For whisper monologues: no bed music, or a low piano pad at -18dB.
- For b-roll essays: licensed ambient (Tom Rosenthal, Ólafur Arnalds, Hania Rani style). Avoid trending TikTok pop hooks — they cheapen the literary register.
- One "soft trend" per month is fine — pick a trending audio that has emotional valence (a slow piano cover, a folk song) and use it for a single piece of cornerstone content.

### Posting frequency & global timing

- **3–5 Reels per week.** Three is the floor; five is the ceiling before quality degrades.
- Best windows: **Tuesday–Thursday, 9–11 a.m. ET** (catches US morning + EU lunch/evening) and a **Sunday 7–9 p.m. ET** "soft post" for the Sunday-scaries audience.
- For a global audience, 12 p.m. ET is the sweet-spot single slot: US East Coast lunch, US West Coast morning, UK evening, Mumbai late-night reflection.

### Hashtag / keyword strategy

Hashtags are now mostly a **topic signal**, not a discovery driver. Use 3–5 per post:

- One broad: `#mentalhealth` or `#emotionalwellness`
- One mid-tail: `#peersupport`, `#emotionalsupport`, `#holdingspace`
- One niche/branded: `#hearthkeepers`, `#pullupachair`
- Plus 1–2 community tags: `#slowliving`, `#interiority`

The keyword in the **first line of the caption** matters more than the hashtag in 2026 — that text is now indexed for in-app search.

### Brands doing this well

- **Brené Brown** — whisper monologue king; quotes-on-cream-paper carousels.
- **Sahaj Kohli / Brown Girl Therapy** — culturally specific carousels paired with reflective Reels.
- **Headspace** (~1M followers) — animated b-roll + meditation voiceover.
- **Calm** — celebrity sleep-story snippets and ambient nature loops.
- **Liz Moody, The Holistic Psychologist, Vienna Pharaon** — long-caption + slow-cut Reel formula.

### Pitfalls — what gets flagged or de-ranked

- Words like "suicide," "self-harm," "kill myself" trigger **sensitive-content downranking** even in a quote card. Use "ending it," "the dark place," or asterisks if context demands.
- Specific clinical claims ("we treat anxiety") will trip Meta's health-content review.
- Linking to external "support" without disclaimers can flag the account.
- Recycled TikTok content with a watermark — auto-suppressed.
- "Trauma-dumping" as content (ungrounded confession with no resolution) is increasingly flagged by users as harmful and tanks save rate.

## 2. Instagram Carousel posts — the long-form save engine

### Algorithm dynamics (2026)

Carousels are quietly the highest-engagement format on Instagram in 2026: **~10% engagement rate vs. 6% for Reels and 7% for single images**. They drive 1.4× wider reach and 22% more saves than single photos. Q1 2026 data shows 5–7 slide carousels generate 3.4× more saves and 2.1× more shares than static images. The algorithm specifically rewards **dwell time, completion rate (% who reach the last slide), and sends/saves per reach** — and it will re-show a carousel to viewers who didn't swipe past slide 1, treating it as a second-chance impression.

Carousels also have a unique "second-life" mechanic: if completion rate stays above ~60%, IG will surface them in Explore weeks after posting.

### Optimal length, aspect ratio, format

- 4:5 portrait (1080×1350) or 1:1 — never landscape; portrait wins ~17% more reach
- **7–10 slides** is the sweet spot
- First slide is 80% of the job — it must be a hook, not a title card
- Last slide is a **soft CTA**: "Save this for the night you'll need it" or "Send this to the friend who's quiet lately"

### The 3 content formats that perform best

1. **The literary essay carousel.** 8–10 slides. Each slide one sentence or short paragraph in a serif font on cream. Slow, paced, designed to read like a chapbook. Anchor of Hearth's grid.
2. **The "field guide."** "10 things to say instead of 'let me know if you need anything'" / "A guide to sitting with a friend in grief." High utility, infinite save value.
3. **The illustrated micro-story.** A single tender vignette (200–300 words) broken across slides with a recurring visual motif (a chair, a candle, a window). Closer to picture-book than infographic.

### Hook patterns (slide 1)

- "Things nobody tells you about being the strong friend."
- "How to tell if you've been carrying it alone for too long."
- "A letter for the night you can't sleep."
- "If you grew up being the parent in the room…"
- "Save this for a 2 a.m. you."
- The slide-1 hook should make the viewer feel **named, not sold to**.

### Audio / music conventions

Carousels can carry audio in 2026 — adding a soft instrumental loop measurably boosts dwell time. Use the same audio palette as Reels for consistency.

### Posting frequency & global timing

- **2–3 carousels per week**; one of those should be your "cornerstone essay" of the week.
- Best window: **Tuesday or Wednesday, 11 a.m. ET** — the carousel rewards being read, not just scrolled, so lunch hours dominate.

### Hashtag / keyword strategy

Same approach as Reels. Carousels benefit even more from a keyword-rich first sentence in the caption (this is what surfaces them in IG search).

### Brands doing this well

- **Sahaj Kohli / Brown Girl Therapy** — text-heavy carousels are the entire grid.
- **The Holistic Psychologist (Nicole LePera)** — 10-slide reframes that average 50–100k saves.
- **Morgan Harper Nichols** — illustrated quote carousels.
- **On Being / Krista Tippett** — quote-driven with poet attribution.
- **The School of Life** — philosophy carousels with a consistent visual language.

### Pitfalls

- Diagnostic / list-format trauma content ("5 signs you have CPTSD") gets flagged and is increasingly seen as harmful pop-psychology. Hearth's voice should never moonlight as DSM-lite.
- Slides that look like ads (CTA-heavy, product shot) tank completion.
- Inconsistent fonts/colors across the grid — the new IG profile design rewards visual cohesion.

## 3. TikTok — for emotional reach, with caution

### Algorithm dynamics (2026)

TikTok's 2026 FYP still rewards **completion rate, rewatches, and shares** above all else, but the platform has tightened mental-health content moderation considerably after the 2024–25 regulatory wave. The algorithm now distinguishes between "supportive mental-health content" and "potentially harmful mental-health content" using both NLP and audio fingerprinting. Supportive content gets normal distribution; flagged content is quietly capped at sub-1k views. There is no warning — you simply notice your reach collapse.

### Optimal length, aspect ratio, format

- 9:16 vertical, 1080×1920
- 21–34 seconds is the current sweet spot for completion
- 60–90 seconds works for storytime monologues if the hook holds
- Captions burned in; TikTok's auto-captions are good enough to use as a base

### The 3 content formats that perform best

1. **POV / storytime monologue.** Founder talks straight to camera, walking, no edits. "POV: you're the friend everyone calls when their world is falling apart." Works because it feels like a voice memo, not a brand.
2. **Talking-with-text overlay.** Camera on a tender moment (pouring tea, lighting a candle), creator does voiceover, key sentences appear as text. The "text + ASMR + voice" stack is the highest-completion format for emotional content right now.
3. **The duet/stitch reply.** Reply to another creator's vulnerable post with Hearth's voice — adds context, doesn't perform. This is how Hearth becomes part of the conversation without paying the discovery tax.

### Hook patterns (first 3 seconds)

- "I had to learn this the hard way…" + face on-camera
- "My therapist said one thing that changed how I do grief."
- "If you grew up parentified, this one's for you."
- Visual hook: someone being handed a mug; someone pulling out a chair across from camera; a single phrase typed live on screen.
- Avoid the "Wait for it..." TikTok trope — it reads as lower-register and clashes with Hearth's literary tone.

### Audio / music conventions

- TikTok heavily rewards trending sounds — but for sensitive content, pick **slow trending sounds** (ambient piano, lo-fi, a folk cover trending in the wellness niche).
- Original sound matters most for serial creators — your own voice, used repeatedly, becomes a sonic signature.
- Avoid "sad-girl" trending sounds tied to suicide/self-harm communities. Algorithm guilt-by-association is real.

### Posting frequency & global timing

- **4–7 posts per week** for growth phase. TikTok rewards volume more than IG does.
- Best windows: **Tuesday–Thursday, 2–6 p.m. local time.** For wellness specifically: **Wednesday 11 a.m.–7 p.m. ET** is the documented hottest window — mid-week burnout drives the audience to seek out emotional content.
- Sunday 7–10 p.m. ET is the second-best slot for emotional/reflective content.

### Hashtag / keyword strategy

3–5 hashtags total: 1 broad (`#mentalhealth`, ~30B views), 2 mid-tail (`#peersupport`, `#emotionalwellness`), 1–2 niche (`#hearthkeepers`). The **caption keyword** is now the dominant discoverability lever — TikTok Search is the new SEO.

### Brands doing this well

- **Liz Moody, Dr. Julie Smith, The Holistic Psychologist** — talking-head + caption.
- **BetterHelp** — heavy paid plus organic UGC; not a model to copy stylistically (clinical, not literary).
- **Calm** — sleep-story snippets dominate.
- **Diary of a CEO clips** (Steven Bartlett) — long-form podcast clipped to 60-second emotional beats.

### Pitfalls

- Mentioning "therapy," "therapist," "depression," "anxiety" repeatedly in one video can trigger sensitive-content review. Hearth's "Keeper, not therapist" framing is actually a *moderation advantage* here — lean on it.
- Hashtag overuse (>6) tanks reach.
- "Algospeak" (using "unalive" etc.) signals you're in a flagged niche; use natural language but keep it grounded.
- Affiliate or referral links in bio without proper disclosure can suspend the account.

## 4. YouTube Shorts — the underrated discovery engine

### Algorithm dynamics (2026)

Shorts hit **90 billion daily views** in 2026 and run on a fully separate ranking system from long-form. The dominant signals are: **viewed-vs-swiped-away ratio in first 3 seconds, average view duration (looped Shorts can exceed 100% AVD, which is rocket fuel), and the post-view behavior** (does the user keep watching YouTube? Subscribe? Visit your channel?). Unlike Reels, Shorts rewards search optimization — tutorials, "how to," and "why" Shorts now compete in YouTube Search itself. The January 2026 update emphasized **topic consistency**: erratic channels get hidden.

### Optimal length, aspect ratio, format

- 9:16, up to 60 seconds (180s now allowed but still under-tested)
- 25–45 seconds is the retention sweet spot
- Loop endings religiously
- Title (now visible above the Short) matters — it's a search signal

### The 3 content formats that perform best for emotional / wellness brands

1. **The "one question" Short.** "Why does no one tell you that grief comes back?" 35-second answer to camera. High searchability.
2. **The repurposed long-form clip.** Pull a 45-second emotional beat from a YouTube long-form, post as Short with a "watch the full essay →" pin. This is the Shorts→long-form flywheel YouTube actively rewards.
3. **The voiceover-on-b-roll mini-essay.** Same as Reels format 3, but with a search-optimized title like "What it really means to hold space for someone."

### Hook patterns (first 3 seconds)

- Open on a question on screen + voice: "Why do we apologize for crying?"
- Pattern interrupt: silence + a single image + a slow line.
- "I learned this from a 78-year-old hospice volunteer…"
- Title overlay must mirror the spoken hook; YouTube cross-references both.

### Audio / music conventions

- YouTube's licensed audio library is the safest path; original VO + a low piano bed performs.
- Trending audio matters less on Shorts than on TikTok — search/title weight is higher.

### Posting frequency & global timing

- **3 Shorts per week**, ideally repurposed from Reels/TikTok to keep production cost flat.
- Best window: **Friday–Sunday 5–9 p.m. ET** (YouTube's audience leans more weekend/evening than TikTok's).

### Hashtag / keyword strategy

The **title** does the heavy lifting. Use 2–3 hashtags max in the description (`#emotionalsupport`, `#peersupport`, `#mentalhealth`). Front-load the keyword in the title.

### Brands doing this well

- **Mel Robbins** — Shorts are direct podcast pulls with a single emotional line; she gets 10M+ on stand-out clips.
- **Diary of a CEO** — the Shorts→long-form flywheel exemplar.
- **Therapy in a Nutshell, Heidi Priebe** — long-form-first creators using Shorts as a top of funnel.
- **Brené Brown** — clips from her Netflix specials and HBR talks.

### Pitfalls

- Disjointed channels (Shorts about meditation + a vlog + a recipe) confuse the topic classifier.
- "Bait-and-switch" Shorts (hook promises X, content delivers Y) get retention-flagged faster than on TikTok.
- Shorts that link to non-YouTube URLs get reach-capped.

## 5. YouTube Long-Form — the slow brand-building engine

### Algorithm dynamics (2026)

Long-form on YouTube in 2026 rewards **session time** (does watching this video keep the user on YouTube?) and **audience retention curves**. Mid-length videos (5–10 min) lead retention at ~31.5%, but **the 12–18 minute essay window is where loyalty compounds** — past minute 8, YouTube starts placing your videos in higher-value suggested slots. 55%+ of viewers drop in the first minute, so the cold-open is everything.

This is the Hearth crown-jewel format: a 12–18 minute literary essay-with-images that lives forever, gets searched, gets embedded, and feeds every other channel for weeks.

### Optimal length, aspect ratio, format

- 16:9, 1920×1080 minimum, 4K preferred for the slow b-roll aesthetic
- 8–18 minutes for narrative essays; 18–25 minutes for interviews with Keepers / members
- Channel branding in intro: ≤3 seconds. Move logos to end card.
- Chapter markers required (helps both retention and search)

### The 3 content formats that perform best

1. **The literary essay.** Founder narrates over slow b-roll: "On the loneliness of being the strong friend." 12–14 minutes. Closing thought leaves space, not closure. This is Hearth's own version of what Nerdwriter and The School of Life do for film and philosophy.
2. **The Keeper conversation.** 25-minute, two-camera, soft-lit conversation between founder and a Keeper. Long-form intimacy. Great for repurposing into 8–10 Shorts.
3. **The member story (with consent).** A Hearth member tells their story of joining, in their own words, on their own couch. Documentary register. Builds the brand's emotional credibility.

### Hook patterns (first 60 seconds)

- Cold open with a tender visual + a single line: "She told me she hadn't cried in eleven years."
- Open on a question the audience has been carrying: "What do you do when no one in your life has the bandwidth to hear you?"
- Avoid "Hey friends, in today's video..." — kills retention 30 seconds in.
- 0–3 minute pacing: visual reset every 10–20 seconds. After minute 3, slow to 25–40 second cuts.

### Audio / music conventions

- A licensed score for each video. Build a sonic identity (one composer, three or four tracks rotated). Hania Rani / Ólafur Arnalds / Nils Frahm-adjacent.
- Voice-over is the brand. Record in a treated room. The voice is Hearth.
- Avoid stock music that appears in other wellness brands' videos.

### Posting frequency & global timing

- **One long-form per week**, max. Two per month is fine for a brand-building channel.
- Publish **Tuesday or Thursday 12 p.m. ET** — catches the global lunch crowd.

### Hashtag / keyword strategy

Title is everything. Format: `[Tender keyword phrase] | Hearth`. Description should include 150–200 words of search-rich context, three timestamped chapters, and a soft CTA to the membership. Tags: 5–8, mixing branded and broad.

### Brands doing this well

- **The School of Life** — the gold standard for tender, literary YouTube.
- **On Being / Krista Tippett** — interview format done with care.
- **Mel Robbins, Brené Brown, Dr. Julie Smith** — founder-led essay channels.
- **Diary of a CEO** — interview-driven, but the editing rhythm is worth studying.
- **Yung Pueblo (Diego Perez)** — slow-paced reflection videos hitting six figures.

### Pitfalls

- Demonetization risk: detailed mention of self-harm or suicide can yellow-icon a video. Use the "988" tone-tag at the start of any heavy episode.
- Slow intros without a hook — terminal.
- Forgetting end-screens — you lose the session-time bonus.

## 6. LinkedIn video — the founder's channel

### Algorithm dynamics (2026)

LinkedIn's 2026 algorithm has been radically rewritten around **dwell time and educational depth**, with a heavy bias toward **founder-led content over company-page content**. Founder profiles consistently outreach company pages by 5–10×. **Document/PDF carousels lead all formats at 6.6% engagement.** Native video sees 5× higher interaction than text, but only if completion is strong — videos under 30s have 200% better completion than longer cuts. External links cost ~60% reach. Comments-with-substance (>12 words) weight more than likes.

### Optimal length, aspect ratio, format

- Square (1:1) or vertical (4:5/9:16) — square is the safest for the desktop+mobile mix
- 30–90 seconds for discovery; 2–5 minutes for warmer audiences
- Captions burned in (LinkedIn's auto-captions are weaker than Meta's)
- PDF carousels: 8–12 pages, designed for the LinkedIn document viewer

### The 3 content formats that perform best

1. **Founder-to-camera reflection.** The Hearth founder, not in branded gear, reflecting on what running a peer-support company has taught them about emotional labor, capitalism, friendship. Personal, not pitchy.
2. **PDF carousel essay.** Same content as the IG carousel, redesigned for LinkedIn's 4:5 doc viewer. "What I learned pairing 1,200 strangers with someone to talk to."
3. **Behind-the-scenes operations post.** "How we train Keepers" — a 90-second video walking through a Keeper's onboarding day. B2B-style transparency that B2C wellness audiences are increasingly hungry for.

### Hook patterns (first 3 seconds / first line of post)

- "Three years ago I was on the floor of a hotel bathroom. Today I run a company that exists because of that night."
- "We just hit 1,000 members. Here's what I didn't expect."
- "I get one question more than any other from new Keepers..."
- First line of caption is the hook — LinkedIn truncates aggressively.

### Audio / music conventions

LinkedIn audiences watch with sound off ~80% of the time. Captions are non-negotiable. If using bed music, keep it minimal — LinkedIn is a low-music-tolerance environment.

### Posting frequency & global timing

- **2–3 posts per week** on the founder profile; 1 per week on the Hearth company page (mostly to push founder posts).
- Best windows: **Tuesday/Wednesday/Thursday, 8–10 a.m. ET.** Avoid Friday after 1 p.m. and weekends.

### Hashtag / keyword strategy

3–5 hashtags: `#mentalhealth`, `#leadership`, `#founderjourney`, `#emotionalwellness`, plus one branded `#hearthkeepers`.

### Brands / founders doing this well

- **Whitney Wolfe Herd (Bumble)** — founder-led emotional-vulnerability template.
- **Adam Grant** — research + reflection.
- **Simon Sinek** — short video clips with moral force.
- **Liz Fosslien** — visual essay carousels for emotion-at-work.
- **Arianna Huffington (Thrive)** — wellness-at-work crossover.

### Pitfalls

- Anything that reads as "personal-brand humblebrag" tanks. LinkedIn's audience smells it.
- Pitching the membership directly will limit reach — soft CTAs only ("DM me 'chair' for early access").
- External links in the post body — move them to the comments.

## 7. Twitter / X — the founder's thought-leadership lab

### Algorithm dynamics (2026)

X's 2026 algorithm now weights **time-on-platform** heavily — does your post send users into replies, click-throughs, embedded video, or your profile? Long-form posts (up to 25,000 chars for Premium) and threads with narrative arc are amplified. **Mental-health-specific content has been algorithmically de-emphasized** following 2024–25 regulatory pressure. Grok now scores post tone — positive/constructive content gets wider distribution, negative/combative content is reach-capped even with high engagement.

This is a difficult platform for Hearth's content, but a useful platform for the **founder's voice**.

### Optimal length, aspect ratio, format

- 200–280 characters for single tweets (sweet spot for completion)
- Threads of 5–9 posts work best
- Native video: 9:16 or 1:1, 45–90 seconds
- Embedded images get more dwell than plain text

### The 3 content formats that perform best

1. **The aphorism.** A single, well-cut sentence from one of Hearth's longer essays. "The strong friend is the one who hasn't been asked how she's doing in nine months."
2. **The thread-essay.** A 7-tweet thread distilling a YouTube long-form. Each tweet a paragraph. Last tweet is a soft CTA.
3. **The reply.** The founder replies thoughtfully to wellness-adjacent thinkers (Yung Pueblo, Esther Perel, Dr. Becky, Vienna Pharaon). Replies travel further than original posts in 2026.

### Hook patterns (first 3 seconds — i.e., the first 8 words)

- "There is a particular loneliness in…"
- "Most people don't know that grief…"
- "I used to think strength meant…"
- A question that the reader has secretly been carrying.

### Audio / music conventions

Video on X is 90%+ sound-off. Captions critical.

### Posting frequency & global timing

- **1–2 posts per day on the founder account.** X is a daily-presence platform.
- Best windows: **8–10 a.m. ET and 8–10 p.m. ET.** Evening is increasingly stronger for reflective content.

### Hashtag / keyword strategy

Hashtags are largely cosmetic on X in 2026. Keywords inside the post body matter more.

### Brands / founders doing this well

- **Yung Pueblo** — single-line aphorisms that travel.
- **Esther Perel** — relationship/intimacy threads.
- **Naval** — the template Hearth's founder should study for compression.
- **Dr. Becky Kennedy** — parenting + emotional regulation threads.
- **Ryan Holiday** — book-passage compression.

### Pitfalls

- Using clinical mental-health terminology repeatedly will get the account into Grok's "sensitive" classification.
- Engaging with political/cultural battles tanks the brand's tone score.
- Negative framing ("I hate when people…") is now reach-capped.

## 8. Threads — the early-mover opportunity

### Algorithm dynamics (2026)

Threads hit **400M MAU and ~141M DAU by late 2025**, surpassing X's mobile DAU. Critically, **the platform still gives non-followers significant feed real estate** — an account with zero followers can land on a stranger's For You feed with the right post. Multiple analysts call Q4 2026 the closing window on this; after that, follower-graph-weighting kicks in fully. The algorithm rewards **conversational, personality-driven content over polished broadcast posts** and weights replies, quote-reposts, and dwell time.

### Optimal length, aspect ratio, format

- 80–500 characters for highest engagement
- Posts with one image outperform plain text by ~40%
- Native video: 9:16, 30–90 seconds, but text is still the dominant lane
- Reply chains (your own post, reply to yourself, reply again) build dwell

### The 3 content formats that perform best

1. **The mini-essay.** 4–6 connected posts, each a paragraph. Hero's-journey mini-arc.
2. **The single tender line + quiet image.** "Some days you don't need advice. You need someone to pull up a chair." + one b-roll still.
3. **The question post.** "What's one sentence someone said to you that you've never forgotten?" Threads is the most replyable major platform; questions seed compounding visibility.

### Hook patterns

- Same as X but slightly softer / more conversational.
- "A thought I keep returning to:" works unusually well.
- "This is going to sound small, but..." invites empathy.

### Audio / music conventions

Mostly text-driven. When using video, the IG/Reels conventions apply.

### Posting frequency & global timing

- **1–3 posts per day.** Threads is a high-cadence platform. The cost of posting is low; the algorithmic upside of being present is high.
- Best windows: **7–9 a.m. and 8–10 p.m. ET.**

### Hashtag / keyword strategy

Threads supports one tag per post in 2026. Use it sparingly. Keywords-in-body remain the dominant discovery signal.

### Brands doing this well

- **Cleo Wade** — short tender lines, gigantic engagement.
- **Mari Andrew** — illustrated mini-essays.
- **Yung Pueblo** — same aphorism style as X, ported.
- **Brian Lovin, Casey Newton** — founder-voice template.
- Most therapists and emotional-wellness creators are still under-built here — Hearth has open lanes.

### Pitfalls

- Cross-posting from X verbatim performs poorly — Threads' audience leans warmer.
- Over-formal tone reads as broadcast.
- Linking out kills reach (same as IG).

## 9. Pinterest — the overlooked, high-intent channel

### Algorithm dynamics (2026)

Pinterest is ranked the **#1 platform for self-worth and purpose** by its own users — the polar opposite tonal environment from X or TikTok. It is **explicitly favoring positive, supportive content** in the wellness space; emotional wellbeing is a centerpiece of the 2026 Pinterest Predicts report. The 2026 algorithm prioritizes **intentional discovery** (planners, doers, dreamers) over scroll-time. Health + wellness is a top-5 category with 7B+ pins. Female-skewing audience (~70%), high-intent — Pinterest users are 3× more likely to convert from a save to a purchase than IG users.

### Optimal length, aspect ratio, format

- 2:3 vertical pins, 1000×1500
- Idea Pins (multi-frame video stories), 9:16, up to 60 seconds
- Standard pins remain the workhorse — Idea Pins are a secondary lane

### The 3 content formats that perform best

1. **The quote pin.** A single Hearth line on cream paper, designed to be saved to a "comfort" or "self-care" board.
2. **The "guide" pin.** "10 things to write in a hard-day journal" — links to a Hearth blog/Substack post.
3. **The series board.** A board called "Letters from Hearth" with 30+ pins, each linked to one piece of long-form content. Boards compound over years.

### Hook patterns

- Pinterest hooks live in the **pin title** and the **first line of pin description**.
- "A letter to the strong friend." / "30 things to say at 2 a.m. when you can't sleep."
- Visuals: muted earth tones, serif type, lots of white space — exactly Hearth's brand world.

### Audio / music conventions

Pinterest is overwhelmingly silent. No audio strategy needed.

### Posting frequency & global timing

- **5–10 pins per week.** Pinterest rewards consistency over virality.
- Time-of-day matters less; pins live for months. Schedule a steady drip.

### Keyword strategy

Pinterest is **the keyword-driven platform**. Treat every pin like a mini SEO page:

- Title: keyword-rich ("Letter to the strong friend who's tired")
- Description: 200 characters with 3–4 long-tail keywords
- Board name: keyword-targeted ("emotional support quotes")
- ALT text on the image: filled out

### Brands doing this well

- **The School of Life** — Pinterest is one of their largest non-paid channels.
- **Morgan Harper Nichols** — illustrated quotes are Pinterest gold.
- **Cleo Wade** — same.
- **Calm** — sleep-routine and bedtime pins.

### Pitfalls

- Spammy linking to gated content kills domain quality.
- Mental-health *advice* pins (vs. emotional-companion pins) are increasingly fact-checked.
- Pins linking to broken landing pages tank the account.

## 10. Substack / Newsletter — the owned channel ("Embers")

### Algorithm dynamics (2026)

Substack is no longer just an email list — it's now a content network. **32 million new subscribers came from inside the Substack app in a single quarter** in late 2025. The Notes feed, Recommendations engine, and Chat make Substack a discovery platform with a built-in conversion funnel. Notes are the dominant new-subscriber driver; **20–30 minutes/day on Notes drives ~70% of new subscribers** for serious writers. Wellness, spirituality, and mental-health categories are among the fastest-growing.

This is Hearth's **owned, durable** channel — and the natural home for the "Embers" newsletter.

### Optimal length, aspect ratio, format

- Email newsletter: 800–1,400 words for a weekly cadence, 2,500+ for a monthly deep essay
- Always include 1 image (boosts open rate ~12%)
- Audio version (Substack supports it natively in 2026) — record the founder reading the essay; this becomes a podcast feed too
- Notes: 3–8 lines, micro-essay format

### What "Embers" should be

A weekly Sunday-evening letter, 900–1,200 words. Each issue:

1. A literary essay on a single emotional theme (loneliness, the strong friend, being parentified, ambient grief, the friend you stopped calling).
2. A "small thing" — one gentle invitation. "This week, try writing one sentence to the person you almost called."
3. A "from the chairs" — one anonymized snippet (with consent) from a Keeper conversation. The most valuable column on the page.
4. A soft CTA: "If you're ready to pull up a chair…" linking to membership.

Free tier: full essay + first invitation. Paid tier ($39/mo aligned with Hearth membership): "From the chairs" archive, audio version, founder Q&A in Chat.

### The 3 content formats that perform best

1. **The Sunday literary essay** (the spine).
2. **The mid-week Note thread** — 4 to 6 short Notes that re-package one idea from the upcoming essay. This is the discovery driver.
3. **The audio essay** — same essay, founder reading it, 10–12 minutes. Doubles as a podcast.

### Hook patterns (subject lines)

- "A letter for the night you can't sleep."
- "What we've learned from 1,000 chairs."
- "The friend you stopped calling."
- "Three things I never said to my mother."
- Subject lines should be **promises, not previews**. ~60% open rate is achievable in this niche.

### Posting frequency & global timing

- **Sunday 7 p.m. ET** for the main letter (catches Sunday-scaries audience globally).
- **2–3 Notes per day** (5–7 min each). This is the new minimum for Substack growth.
- One audio episode per week, dropped Tuesday 7 a.m. ET.

### Keyword / discovery strategy

- Cross-recommend with adjacent newsletters (this is the Substack growth lever): On Being's newsletter, Cup of Jo, Yung Pueblo, Heather Havrilesky's Ask Polly, Mari Andrew, Anne Helen Petersen.
- Notes that *quote your own post* and add a single new line drive huge subscribe-from-Notes conversions.

### Newsletters / writers doing this well

- **Heather Havrilesky / Ask Polly** — literary advice column.
- **Anne Helen Petersen / Culture Study** — long-form essay rhythm.
- **Mari Andrew / Out of the Blue** — illustrated emotional micro-essays.
- **Cup of Jo** (different platform but template applies).
- **Yung Pueblo's Substack** — meditation-adjacent.

### Pitfalls

- Too-frequent paywalled posts erode trust on a sensitive-content brand.
- Welcome sequences that immediately upsell the membership feel transactional. Lead with three free essays before any soft pitch.
- Forgetting to mark Embers as a newsletter that "is not a substitute for therapy" — required disclaimer, but write it warmly, not legally.

# Hearth platform strategy — synthesis

## The 3 platforms to prioritize at launch (Phase 1, months 0–6)

### 1. Substack (Embers) — the spine

The owned, durable channel. Every other platform exists to drive subscribers here. Embers is where Hearth's literary voice lives without algorithmic compromise. It is also the lowest-risk channel for a sensitive product — no shadowbans, no de-ranking, no platform whim. The conversion path is: read 4–8 essays → subscribe to Hearth membership.

### 2. Instagram (Reels + Carousels together)

Instagram is the **single highest-leverage discovery platform** for Hearth's exact aesthetic and audience. The combination of Reels (top-of-funnel) and Carousels (save-driven, deep engagement) lets one piece of content do two jobs. This is where Brené Brown, Sahaj Kohli, and the entire emotional-wellness category live. Goal: 50k followers by month 9.

### 3. YouTube Long-Form

The slowest-burning, most defensible channel. A 14-minute literary essay on Hearth's channel will be discoverable for years. It feeds Substack (audio version), feeds Reels/TikTok/Shorts (clipped), and is the format that most resembles what Hearth *is* — a long, slow conversation. Cadence: 1 long-form per week, 2 per month is acceptable.

## Platforms to deprioritize until Phase 2 (months 6–12)

- **TikTok** — the moderation environment for sensitive mental-health content is hostile in 2026. Wait until Hearth has an established voice and a hardened legal/clinical disclaimer footprint before entering.
- **YouTube Shorts** — repurpose only. Don't produce Shorts-first content.
- **LinkedIn** — turn on once the founder has a story to tell about company growth (~month 9). Then run founder-led, not company-page.
- **X / Twitter** — founder presence only, low cadence, no brand-account.
- **Threads** — the Phase-1.5 dark horse. If bandwidth allows, turn on by month 4 — the early-mover window closes by Q4 2026.
- **Pinterest** — turn on at month 6 with a 90-day batch of pins. Set-and-forget compounder.

## Phase 2 (months 6–18): the four-channel expansion

In order: Threads, Pinterest, LinkedIn (founder), TikTok. Each gets a 60-day pilot with a single dedicated repurposer.

Every week, **one piece of atomic content** is produced. Everything else is atomization.

### The atom: one Hearth Essay

A single 1,000–1,400 word literary essay on one emotional theme. Written by the founder (or a trusted Hearth voice). One theme per week. Produced on Monday.

## Cross-platform tonal rules

1. **Never mention diagnoses.** Hearth is peer support, not clinical care. Every platform should reinforce this.
2. **The Keeper-not-therapist line** is a brand asset *and* a moderation shield. Lean on it.
3. **Soft CTAs only.** "Pull up a chair." "If you're ready, the door's open." Never "Sign up now."
4. **Disclaimers, warmly written.** Every Substack and YouTube long-form should have a short, human note about crisis resources, written in Hearth's voice — not a legal footer.
5. **The visual world is consistent across platforms.** Cream backgrounds, serif headlines, muted earth tones, slow b-roll, original audio in a small palette of three to four composers.

The unifying idea: Hearth doesn't need to be everywhere loudly. It needs to be a few places quietly, with a voice you can recognize from across the room.
