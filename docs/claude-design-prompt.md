# Hearth — Design Brief for Claude Design

## What Hearth Is

Hearth is a peer-support membership service for adults navigating grief, life transitions, family pressure, identity questions, and the everyday weight of being a person. It lives in the space between "calling a friend" and "seeing a therapist" — the underserved gap where most real emotional demand actually sits.

Members are paired with a **Keeper** — a trained, culturally-fluent companion who listens, reflects, advises, and stays consistent across months and years. Hearth is explicitly **not therapy**. It does not diagnose, treat, or prescribe. When a member needs clinical care, they are routed to **The Bridge** — a curated network of licensed therapists.

Hearth's primary audience is diaspora communities in the US and Canada — people from cultures where the village elder, trusted aunt, or community wise-person tradition is still alive in memory but absent in modern life. Hearth restores that relationship as a modern service.

## Brand Voice and Vocabulary

Hearth uses its own vocabulary consistently across every surface. This language is the brand's moat and must appear exactly as written:

| Concept | Hearth word |
|---|---|
| The service/brand | Hearth |
| The provider/companion | Keeper |
| A 1:1 video session | A Sit |
| The async chat thread | The Long Talk |
| A group program | Circle |
| The peer community space | The Common Room |
| Short content / micro-wisdom pieces | Embers |
| The clinical referral path | The Bridge |
| The onboarding intake flow | The First Sit |
| The member-Keeper matching process | Pairing |
| A Keeper's public profile page | At the Hearth |
| Member testimonials | The Stories |
| Gifting a membership | Light a Hearth |
| Standard tier | Hearthside |
| Premium tier | Hearth Deep |

**Voice:** Warm but not saccharine. Confident but not preachy. Uses words like: gather, sit, hold, carry, weight, listen, tend. Avoids: journey, growth, mindful, heal, transform, manifest, optimize, unlock.

**Sample phrases Hearth uses:**
- "Pull up to the Hearth"
- "Bring it to the Hearth"
- "You weren't meant to carry it alone"
- "Some things deserve the long talk"

**What Hearth never says:** "Begin your healing journey" / "Unlock your best self" / "Mindful living starts here"

## The Core Service Features

### The Sit (1:1 Video Session)
- 35–60 minute video call between member and their Keeper
- Biweekly cadence (twice a month, every 2 weeks) on Hearthside; weekly cadence (4/month) on Hearth Deep
- Has a default structure: arrival → long listening → pattern noticing → closing ritual
- Not recorded by default; member can opt in to recording for personal playback
- Keeper may take private notes (encrypted, member-readable)

### The Long Talk (Async Chat)
- Persistent chat thread between member and their Keeper
- Keeper responds within 24 hours on Hearthside; within 4 hours on Hearth Deep
- Supports text, voice notes, photos, and links
- Every Friday, Keeper sends a "what stayed with me from this week" reflection — this is a brand signature
- Not an always-on messaging app; the brand encourages gathering thoughts

### Circles (Group Programs)
- 4–8 week cohorts of 6–10 members on a shared theme
- Examples: Diaspora Mothers Circle, Caregiving Circle, Grief Circle, Wedding Season Circle, Career-Cultural Circle, First-Year-After Circle
- 1 Circle/month included in both tiers; single-Circle passes available as add-ons
- Facilitated by a Keeper, not a clinician

### Embers (Content Library)
- Short written and audio pieces (3–7 minutes each)
- Categories: Heavy Things, Family Stuff, The In-Between, Practical Ground, Rituals
- Written by Keepers and contributing essayists with cultural specificity
- Not generic wellness content

### The Bridge (Clinical Referral)
- When a member's needs exceed peer support, their Keeper facilitates a warm handoff
- Connects members to US-licensed South Asian-heritage and culturally-fluent therapists
- Available as a standalone referral or as part of Hearth Deep tier
- Members can use insurance via partner platforms where applicable

### The First Sit (Onboarding Intake)
- Required before Keeper matching
- Includes PHQ-9 (9 questions), GAD-7 (7 questions), and a custom Hearth screen
- High-risk scores route directly to The Bridge; cannot proceed to Keeper-only tier
- Mid-range scores match with Keeper plus a Bridge recommendation
- Below-threshold scores proceed to Keeper Pairing

## Pricing

| Tier | Price | What's included |
|---|---|---|
| **Hearthside** | $39/month (biweekly Sits) or $390/year (~$32.50/mo, "2 months free") | Matched Keeper, 2 Sits/month (every 2 weeks), unlimited Long Talk, full Embers access, 1 Circle/month |
| **Hearth Deep** | $99/month (weekly Sits) or $990/year (~$82.50/mo, "2 months free") | 4 Sits/month (every week), priority Long Talk (4hr response), 2 Circles/month, anniversary rituals, Bridge therapist matching |

**À la carte add-ons:**
- Extra Sit: $25
- Couples Sit: $45
- Single Circle pass: $25
- Light a Hearth (gift one month of Hearthside): $39

**No free trial. No free/Pull Up tier.** Instead: 14-day money-back guarantee — if the first Sit doesn't feel right, full refund.

The product has three surfaces: (1) the public marketing website, (2) the authenticated member app, and (3) the authenticated Keeper portal.

## Surface 1 — Public Marketing Website (13 pages)

### Page 1: Home
The primary landing page. Must communicate what Hearth is in the first screen — "the space between a friend and a therapist." Conveys the Keeper relationship, the warmth of the service, and the brand origin story. Primary CTA is to begin the intake/signup. Secondary CTAs: see pricing, browse Keepers, read Embers. Must include a brief what-is-a-Keeper section, a Circles preview, and member testimonials (The Stories). Closes with a footer.

### Page 2: How It Works
Walks a prospective member through the full journey in sequence: the First Sit (intake), Pairing (Keeper matching), the weekly Sit, the Long Talk, Circles, and the Embers library. Explains what a Keeper is and is not. Explains The Bridge. Explains the safety net (14-day money-back). Ends with CTA to begin.

### Page 3: Meet the Keepers
A browsable directory of Keeper profiles. Each Keeper card shows: name, photo, brief bio, the themes they hold (grief, career transitions, parenting, etc.), languages spoken, and a link to their full "At the Hearth" profile page. Includes a filter by theme and language. Communicates that Keepers are rooted in cultures where the elder tradition is alive.

### Page 4: At the Hearth (individual Keeper profile)
A Keeper's public profile page. Includes: full name and photo, a short personal statement in their own voice, the themes they work with, languages spoken, their background (not credentials — lived experience), and member testimonials attributed to them. CTA to be paired with this Keeper, or to begin intake and be matched. This page has a template that applies to all Keepers.

### Page 5: Pricing
The full pricing page. Displays Hearthside and Hearth Deep tiers side by side with full feature breakdowns. Includes à la carte add-ons. Explains the annual prepay discount. Explains "Light a Hearth" gifting. Explains the 14-day money-back guarantee prominently. Addresses the most common objection: "Is this therapy?" with a clear, non-defensive answer. FAQ accordion below the pricing table.

### Page 6: Circles
Explains what Circles are (group programs, not group therapy). Lists current and upcoming cohorts with their themes, dates, Keeper facilitator, and number of spots remaining. Explains who Circles are for. Explains the cohort format (4–8 weeks, 6–10 members). CTAs: join a Circle as a member, or buy a single-Circle pass as a non-member.

### Page 7: Embers
A preview of the Embers content library. Shows the five categories (Heavy Things, Family Stuff, The In-Between, Practical Ground, Rituals). Displays a selection of featured pieces with titles, brief descriptions, and estimated read/listen time. Full access requires a membership. The page should communicate the tone and depth of Embers content — not generic wellness, but specific and adult.

### Page 8: The Bridge
Explains what The Bridge is and why it exists. Communicates that Hearth is peer support, not therapy — and that knowing the difference is a feature, not a limitation. Explains the routing process (intake screener, Keeper recognition, warm handoff). Lists the types of situations The Bridge handles. Does not list individual therapists publicly. Ends with reassurance that being routed to The Bridge is not a failure or a dismissal.

### Page 9: About / Our Story
The brand origin story. Tells the founding story in full: the village elder tradition, how modern life stripped it away, and why Hearth exists. Introduces the founder and their background. States the mission and values plainly. This page should feel like a letter, not a corporate about page. Includes the vocabulary of the brand (warmth, weight, carry, gather). No corporate speak.

### Page 10: The Stories (Testimonials)
A dedicated testimonials page. Member stories, attributed to first name and theme (e.g. "Ayesha, navigating grief"). Mix of short pull-quotes and longer written pieces. May include audio testimonials if available. Represents the range of life situations Hearth serves: grief, career, family pressure, diaspora identity, caregiving, transitions.

### Page 11: The Tea (Free Content / Blog)
Hearth's free public content arm. Daily or weekly short-form pieces from Keepers: 5-minute audio perspectives, short written essays, micro-wisdom. Browsable by category. Serves as a discovery channel and brand voice expression. Not paywalled. Links to the full Embers library for members. Has its own identity within the Hearth brand.

### Page 12: Privacy Policy
Legal page. Written in plain, human language consistent with Hearth's brand voice — not boilerplate legalese. Explains what data is collected, how it is stored (HIPAA-comparable, encrypted, US-based), what is never shared (especially with advertising platforms), and how to request deletion. Explicitly states no data is sold.

### Page 13: Terms of Service
Legal page. States clearly and prominently that Hearth is peer support, not therapy, not medical advice, and no doctor-patient relationship exists. Covers subscription terms, cancellation, refund policy (14-day money-back), intellectual property, and acceptable use. Written in plain language.

## Surface 2 — Member App, Authenticated (12 pages)

### Page 14: Dashboard / Home (Member)
The member's home screen after login. Shows: upcoming Sit (date, time, Keeper name), recent Long Talk messages, any active Circle, and a featured Embers piece. Quick actions: message Keeper, book a Sit, browse Circles. Surfaces the week's Friday reflection from the Keeper if available. Feels like arriving home, not opening a dashboard.

### Page 15: My Keeper
The dedicated page for the member's relationship with their Keeper. Shows the Keeper's full profile, the member's relationship history (when they paired, how many Sits completed), upcoming and past Sits with notes the member has access to, and a direct link to the Long Talk. Option to re-pair if the relationship isn't working. The emotional center of the member's app experience.

### Page 16: The Long Talk (Chat)
The async chat thread between member and Keeper. Supports text messages, voice note recording and playback, photo sharing, and links. Shows Keeper's response window (e.g., "responds within 24 hours"). Displays the Friday reflection prominently when it arrives. The chat should feel slow and intentional — not like WhatsApp or iMessage.

### Page 17: Book a Sit (Scheduling)
A calendar view of the member's Keeper's available time slots. Member selects a time, confirms, and receives a reminder. Shows the member's upcoming and past Sits. Includes an option to indicate what they want to bring to the Sit (optional free text, visible only to member and Keeper). Option to add an extra Sit as a one-off purchase ($35).

### Page 18: The Sit — Live Video Session
The in-session screen during a live Sit. Shows video feed of member and Keeper. Timer running. Option to switch to audio-only. Option to enable recording (member consent). Option to share a note or reminder to self during the session. Minimal distractions — the session is the focus.

### Page 19: Circles
Browsable list of available and active Circles. Shows theme, Keeper facilitator, cohort dates, number of spots remaining, and a brief description. Member can join upcoming Circles (within their tier's included Circles or via single-Circle pass). Active Circles show meeting schedule and a shared space for that cohort. Past Circles are archived and visible to participants.

### Page 20: Embers Library
Full access to the Embers content library for members. Browsable by category (Heavy Things, Family Stuff, The In-Between, Practical Ground, Rituals). Each piece has a title, brief description, read/listen time, and the Keeper or essayist who wrote it. Supports saved/favorited pieces. Audio pieces have a player. Searchable.

### Page 21: The Bridge
Visible to all members; active for members whose Keeper has initiated a Bridge referral or who have high intake scores. Shows an explanation of The Bridge, the member's referral status, and if applicable, the matched therapist's name, credentials, and how to book. Links to external booking via Headway/Alma where applicable. Does not feel alarming or clinical — feels like a warm next step.

### Page 22: Account Settings
Member profile: name, photo, language preferences, notification settings, timezone. Privacy settings: whether session notes are visible to member, whether to opt in to session recording. Keeper preferences: option to flag themes that are off-limits for discussion. Accessibility settings.

### Page 23: Billing & Subscription
Current plan (Hearthside or Hearth Deep), billing cycle, next charge date. Option to switch tiers. Option to pause (if pause is supported). Option to cancel — must be frictionless per FTC Click-to-Cancel requirement; no dark patterns. Option to apply the 14-day money-back guarantee within the window. History of past charges. "Light a Hearth" — gift a membership to someone else.

### Page 24: Notifications / Activity Feed
Consolidated view of all activity: Keeper replies, upcoming Sit reminders, new Embers pieces, Circle updates, Friday reflections, and system messages. Can be filtered. Allows notification preference management inline.

### Page 25: The Common Room (Community Space)
A peer-led community area for members. Not moderated by Keepers in real time. Organized by theme (similar to Circle themes). Members can post and respond. This is separate from the Keeper relationship — it is a members-only space for peer connection. Clear rules displayed: this is not crisis support; no advice-giving posture required; be present, not prescriptive.

## Surface 3 — Keeper Portal, Authenticated (11 pages)

### Page 26: Keeper Dashboard
The Keeper's home screen. Shows: today's and this week's Sits, unread Long Talk messages across all members (with member name and preview), active Circles, and weekly stats (Sits completed, message response rate, average NPS). Surfaces any flagged members (concerning language in Long Talk, mid-range intake scores, upcoming Bridge referrals). Quick actions: open a Long Talk, start a Sit, write a Friday reflection.

### Page 27: My Members
A list of all active members assigned to this Keeper. Each row shows: member name/photo, last Sit date, next Sit date, Long Talk activity, and any active flags. Clicking a member opens their full profile. Shows relationship health at a glance.

### Page 28: Member Profile / Session Notes
Per-member deep view for the Keeper. Contains: the member's intake screener results (PHQ-9, GAD-7 scores), a chronological log of Sits and notes, the Long Talk thread summary, any Bridge referral status, and the Keeper's private working notes. Notes are encrypted and member-readable (members can request their own notes). This page is the Keeper's professional record for each relationship.

### Page 29: The Long Talk — Keeper View
The Keeper's chat interface. Shows all active Long Talk threads listed by member, sorted by most recent unread. Selecting a thread opens the full conversation. Supports text, voice note recording, photo sharing. Displays each member's response SLA (next response due). Shows when the Friday reflection was last sent, with a prompt to write it if overdue.

### Page 30: Schedule Management
The Keeper's calendar and availability settings. Keeper sets weekly availability windows. Confirmed Sits appear on the calendar. Keeper can block time, mark themselves on leave, and set recurring availability. Shows upcoming Sits for the week and the total Sit load for the month (for compensation calculation reference).

### Page 31: The Sit — Live Video Session (Keeper Side)
Same video session screen as the member's, but with Keeper-specific features: a collapsible note-taking panel (session notes auto-saved to the member's profile), a timer, member's stated intention for the Sit (if they filled it in), and a Bridge referral button if the Keeper determines clinical need arises during the session.

### Page 32: Circles — Keeper View
Management view for Circles the Keeper is facilitating. Shows enrolled members, meeting schedule, attendance, and a shared space for posting updates between sessions. Keeper can add resources for the cohort. Shows upcoming Circle sessions and a count of available spots if the cohort is still open.

### Page 33: Bridge Referral Flow
When a Keeper initiates a Bridge referral for a member, this page guides the Keeper through the process: documenting the reason, selecting from the Bridge therapist network, and sending the warm handoff message to the member. Includes a protocol checklist to ensure the Keeper stays within peer-support scope during the handoff. Referral is logged to the member's profile.

### Page 34: At the Hearth — Keeper Profile Editor
The Keeper's public profile page editor. Keeper writes their personal statement, selects the themes they hold, confirms their languages, and uploads their photo. Preview of how the profile appears to prospective members. This is what prospective members see on the "Meet the Keepers" page and when being paired.

### Page 35: Keeper Earnings & Payouts
Shows the Keeper's earnings breakdown: number of Sits completed, per-Sit rate, bonus eligibility, total earned this month and year to date, and payout history. Keeper can enter or update their payment details. Shows payout schedule. Includes a breakdown by member if needed for the Keeper's own records.

### Page 36: Keeper Settings & Support
Keeper account settings: personal info, notification preferences, timezone, language settings. Access to Hearth's Keeper training materials and protocol documents. Link to the monthly Keeper Circle (peer supervision) schedule. Support contact for escalations. Option to flag a technical issue with a Sit.

## What Hearth Is Not

This context helps avoid wrong-direction design decisions:

- Not a therapy platform. No clinical framing, no sterile medical aesthetics, no white coats, no DSM language.
- Not a chatbot or AI product. The human relationship is the product. AI is invisible infrastructure.
- Not a social network. Community exists (The Common Room) but connection with a single consistent Keeper is the core.
- Not a productivity or self-improvement app. There are no streaks, badges, or "progress" metrics for members. Hearth is not Duolingo for feelings.
- Not a crisis line. Has crisis protocols and The Bridge, but is not positioned as emergency support.
- Not BetterHelp. BetterHelp rotates therapists, is clinically coded, and has been FTC-settled. Hearth is the opposite of BetterHelp's brand experience.

## The Founding Story (Brand Origin — use wherever the brand needs grounding)

> "In villages, when life got heavy, you walked to the elder's house. There was always a fire, always tea, always a person who'd lived more than you. They didn't have credentials. They had time, attention, and the patience to let your sentence finish.
>
> Modern life has stripped that away. We have therapists for the clinical things and friends for the easy things, but for everything in between — the grief, the half-formed worries, the family questions that don't fit on a couch — most of us have nobody.
>
> Hearth is that elder's house, on your phone, in any language. We are the people who pull up a chair."
