1. THE MARKET BREAKDOWN SYSTEM
   Most founders chase ideas. $100M founders study markets.
   Prompt: "Analyze the market for [your niche]. Do NOT give surface insights.

Break into: TAM, SAM, SOM
Identify top 5 trends shaping demand
Find 5 underserved opportunities
Highlight where money is already flowing

Output as structured insights, not paragraphs."

2. THE PROBLEM PRIORITIZATION ENGINE
   Amateurs solve random problems. Experts solve expensive ones.
   Prompt: "List the top 10 problems in [industry]

Rank by urgency (1-10)
Rank by willingness to pay
Identify which problems are growing fastest
Highlight problems people actively complain about

Output as a ranked table."

3. THE OFFER CREATION FRAMEWORK
   Good products sell. Great offers print money.
   Prompt: "Create a high-converting offer for: [problem]

Define ICP (ideal customer profile)
Craft a clear value proposition
Add pricing strategy (low, mid, premium tiers)
Include guarantee + risk reversal
Show why this beats competitors

Output like a landing page structure."

4. THE DISTRIBUTION DOMINATION PLAN
   No distribution = no business.
   Prompt: "Act like a growth strategist. Build a plan to reach 1M people in 30 days for: [idea]

List top 5 acquisition channels
Define content formats for each
Suggest daily execution plan
Include organic + paid mix
Focus on leverage, not efforts

Output as step-by-step system."

5. THE VIRAL CONTENT ENGINE
   Content is the new distribution.
   Prompt: "Create a viral content strategy for [niche]

Generate 20 high-converting hooks
Suggest 10 content formats
Identify emotional triggers (fear, status, curiosity)
Show why people will share this

Output in repeatable framework."

6. THE SCALE SYSTEM
   Most people hustle. Founders build systems.
   Prompt: "Give me a plan to scale this to 1Cr/month: [describe business]

What to automate first
What to delegate
What systems to build
Hiring roadmap
Bottlenecks that will appear

Output as execution roadmap."
